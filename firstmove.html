First Move

UX Design
July 2019
3 Weeks

Enabling Mobility




Mercedes-Benz has traditionally been an automobile manufacturer. As the mobility landscape changes, so do they.

I designed a new breed of digital assistant with the Innovation Team at Mercedes-Benz.

Background



MB makes great cars — they’re industry leaders for it.

But transportation has already evolved. The ubiquity of ridesharing and generational perceptions of mobility pose challenges that MB doesn’t meet as a traditional OEM.

To keep up with shifting attitudes, we had to dive into more than just cars; we had to re-learn how users got from place to place and the associated challenges.

Challenge




Facilitate mobility for users.


This project was initiated by the Innovation Team at MBUSA.

Our goal was to create a new type of mobility product, leveraging our unfair advantage to attract new and existing customers.

My Role



I led UX design in my team of 3, alongside a software engineer intern and a business intern. I also conducted user research with my teammates.

Overall, we were guided by the three managers of the Innovation Hub team. One of them, David Owen, owned this project and contributed to strategy.

I ran UX research for one week, synthesized insights for another week, and built the design prototypes in the final week.

This project successfully gained approval by our GM in July 2019, received funding, and proceeded into its next stages.

The Problem




Framing Our Investigation



The Innovation Hub team was created on the basis of finding authentic user needs and addressing them. Our process reflected this principle.

While it seemed natural to start with our eventual concept of a monitoring product, our concept was forged through user research.

In particular, the examination of driver relationships with their cars revealed insights into the desires of users.
